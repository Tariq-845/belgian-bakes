Belgian Bakes

About

Belgium has a rich history when it comes to baking, and its traditional bakery products are deeply rooted in the country's culinary heritage.


Belgium is known for its diverse range of bread varieties. Each region has its own specialties, such as the "cougnou" or "bread of Jesus," a sweet bread traditionally eaten during Christmas in Wallonia, and the "pain d'épeautre" or spelt bread in Flanders. Rye, whole wheat, and sourdough breads are also popular choices.


One of Belgium's most famous contributions to the world of baking is the Belgian waffle. While variations of waffles existed in the region for centuries, it wasn't until the 18th century that the modern Brussels and Liège waffles began to take shape. These sweet treats gained international fame and are now enjoyed worldwide.
